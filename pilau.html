East African Pilau

Pilau has a wonderful balance of flavors. It is a festive dish, which is never missing during special occasions or events. Its made with rice cooked in a well-seasoned broth of Meat or chicken. Unlike the Indian pilau, the East African version does not use curry and itâ€™s less spicy. Rice is, without a doubt, one of my favorite food in the world. Not only is it my to-go dish when I need to prepare a quick meal, but it can easily be turned into an elegant dish for special occasions. With the right combination of spices, plain rice can be turned into the main attraction like this Rice Pilau or the West African jollof rice, my go-to one-pot chicken and rice, and much more!

Ingridients

  1. Beef, but chicken or any other type of meat can also be used.
  2. Basmati rice which takes about 20 minutes to cook however, your cooking time may vary depending on the type of rice you use.
  3. 2 Onions
  4. 2 Tomatoes
  5. Garlic
  6. Pilau Masala
  7. Cooking oil
  8. Serano Pepper
  9. Potatoes
  10. Bay leaves
  11. Ginger
  12. Water
  13. Salt
  14. Stock Cubes
  15. cilantro

Method

Step 1

Toast and blend the pilau masala: In a medium saucepan over medium heat.

Step 2

Brown the onions by frying it in oil until it caramelizes. This should take about 10 to 15 minutes. Stir in the Garlic, ginger and serrano pepper.

Step 3

Add the meat stock cubes, pilau masala, cilantro, bay leaves, and salt to taste and brown the meat for about

Step 4

Stir in the tomatoes and cook till it dissolves. Then stir in the potatoes.

Step 5

Add water and bring everything to a rolling boil and leave the potatoes to cook for about 10 minutes.

Step 6

Stir in the rice then cover tightly. You can use an aluminum foil to cover it first, this will help keep the steam in. Then place the lid over it and leave to cook on a low heat for 20 minutes until the liquid has been absorbed.

Step 7

Serve hot! You can sprinkle the rice pilaf with some chopped cilantro, then fluff with a fork. Enjoy.

Note: What Makes Pilau Brown?

The brown color of the pilau comes from caramelizing the onions, browning the meat until it becomes really browned. The pilau seasoning or pilau masala also adds a bit of color to the rice as well as a unique flavor.
